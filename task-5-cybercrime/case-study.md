# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI fraud is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into authorizing payments or sharing sensitive banking details. A common real-life example is the “Jump Deposit Scam” where victims are manipulated into unknowingly authorizing fraudulent transactions. In one reported case, a job seeker in Bengaluru was tricked by a fraudster pretending to be an HR executive from a reputed company. The scammer claimed that the victim’s first salary was being credited but needed “verification.” The victim approved a ₹10,000 request, unknowingly losing the money.


**How the fraud typically happens (step-by-step):**

1.	The scammer sends a Request Money notification to the victim’s UPI app, disguised as a refund, cashback, or salary credit.
2.	If the victim mistakenly believes they are receiving money, they might approve the request without realizing that this action is actually authorizing a debit from their account. 
3.	Instead of receiving funds, money is instantly debited and transferred to the fraudster’s account.

   
**Who is targeted:**

Common targets include students, job seekers, elderly individuals, and people unfamiliar with digital payments. Fraudsters often exploit urgency, trust, or lack of awareness.

**Consequences:**

Victims face immediate financial loss, and due to real-time transactions, recovery is difficult. Fraudsters often move money across multiple accounts quickly, making tracking challenging. 
This case highlights the importance of awareness, as UPI frauds in India have risen significantly with increasing digital transactions.
